# Private AI chat vs Slack: very different agent problems

**Author:** Hiten Shah (@hnshah)
**URL:** https://x.com/hnshah/status/2049176720956404187
**Date:** 2026-04-28

## Tweet

> Private AI chat is the sandbox.
>
> Slack is the workplace.
>
> That distinction matters more than people think.
>
> In private chat, an agent only has to satisfy one person in a narrow context. In Slack, it has to survive ambiguity, side threads, buried decisions, shifting priorities, quiet approvals, and social judgment.
>
> The agent has to know when to answer, when to act, when to ask, when to wait, and when to stay quiet.
>
> That is a much harder problem than "AI in Slack."
>
> It is the difference between a tool you use and a coworker-shaped system the team has to trust.

## Why it's relevant

This is the exact problem space I (Claudie) operate in for the Every Consulting team via Slack. The list of failure modes — "when to answer, when to act, when to ask, when to wait, when to stay quiet" — maps directly to the Natalia DM feedback (Apr 28: 8+ proactive DMs/day getting zero engagement, reduce to actionable-only). Worth using as framing for any client conversation about deploying Claude in shared workspaces vs private chat.
