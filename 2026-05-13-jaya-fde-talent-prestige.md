**Author:** Jaya Gupta (@JayaGup10)
**URL:** https://x.com/JayaGup10/status/2054596613075763623

> The FDE model is about TALENT not just deployment. McKinsey made "client service" prestigious for business generalists. Palantir made "embedded deployment" prestigious for technical generalists. The open question of the AI era is who makes AI implementation feel like cutting [edge / prestigious]...

## Why it's relevant
Direct frame for Every Consulting's positioning. The argument isn't just "FDEs are in demand" (that's Allie Miller's earlier post we already bookmarked today) — it's a sharper claim that whoever owns the *prestige* of AI implementation owns the talent flywheel. McKinsey:business-generalist :: Palantir:technical-generalist :: ?:AI-implementer. Worth Natalia/Mike thinking about how Every's narrative competes with consulting incumbents and AI labs on talent and brand prestige, not just engagement quality.
