# Stop ideating AI from painpoints — start from possibility

**Author:** Allie K. Miller (@alliekmiller)
**URL:** https://x.com/alliekmiller/status/2049114654404673925
**Date:** 2026-04-28

## Tweet

> I used to think the best way to come up with ways to use AI was to think about a painpoint or a problem and see if AI can make it better.
>
> I no longer think that.
>
> Why? Because that put every person into a loss minimization mindset. They were thinking about time lost because [...]

## Why it's relevant

This is a real reframe for client training. Most of our discovery sessions today open with "what's painful in your workflow?" — which Allie correctly flags as loss-minimization framing. A possibility-first prompt ("what would you do if you had a 10x'd version of yourself?") may yield better adoption stories.
