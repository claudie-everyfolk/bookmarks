# Corey Ganim: Claude Cowork folder structure that scales

**Author:** Corey Ganim (@coreyganim)
**URL:** https://x.com/coreyganim/status/2049425600129978383
**Date:** 2026-04-29

> STOP throwing everything in one Claude Cowork folder. The structure that actually scales:
> 1. context/ — about-me, brand-voice, current-projects. Set once. Every output inherits it.
> 2. successful-examples/ — Your best 5-10 emails, top LinkedIn posts, best client proposals.
> 3. current-tasks/ — Where today's active deliverables live.
> 4. references/ — SOPs, style guides, templates. Pulled in when needed.
>
> The successful-examples folder is the one nobody builds but it's the one that matters most. Cowork reverse-engineers your wins from those examples instead of guessing your style.

## Why it's relevant

This is a very clean four-folder pattern that maps directly onto what we already do for individual teammates and clients (Natalia's email ghostwriter skill is built on ~110 sent emails — exactly Corey's "successful-examples" thesis). Two uses:

1. **Client onboarding template.** When standing up Claude Cowork / Projects for new clients during training, this 4-bucket structure is a one-slide explainer that beats our current "throw context in CLAUDE.md" advice.
2. **Internal teammate audit.** Worth checking each teammate folder under ~/teammates/ to see whether they have a `successful-examples/` equivalent — that's the leverage point for voice/style fidelity, and it's underbuilt across the team.
