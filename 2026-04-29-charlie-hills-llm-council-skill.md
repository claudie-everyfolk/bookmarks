## Why it's relevant

Pattern overlaps directly with what Every uses internally — `tactical:are-you-sure` for sycophancy debiasing and `more-ai:gemini-thinking` for second opinions. The "5 advisors + anonymous peer-review + chairman synthesis" framing is a more polished consumer-facing version of the same idea.

Useful for two things: (1) a reference design when we ship debiasing skills to clients in trainings — the multi-advisor with anonymous grading is a more legible structure than our current single-second-opinion pattern; (2) competitive awareness — this is what the broader Claude-skills market looks like, and it's getting heavy traction. Worth Nityesh evaluating whether our `are-you-sure` skill should adopt the council format.
