## Tweet

> Skills might be the single most important abstraction of the decade.
>
> And they aren't limited to agentic coding tools anymore.
>
> You will soon be able to program *everything* using skills.
>
> Every person I know hates their CRM passionately because they have to spend hours glued to [it]...

## Why It's Relevant

This is extremely relevant to us. Skills ARE how we've built Claudie — the entire skill system is what makes Claude Code useful as an always-on employee. Santiago's framing that skills will extend beyond coding tools into CRM, enterprise software, etc. is exactly the trajectory we're riding.

Strong validation of our approach. Could be useful framing for client conversations: "skills are how you teach AI to do your job, not just answer your questions."

- **Natalia** — Sales framing she can use immediately. "Skills are how you teach AI to do your job, not just answer your questions" reframes the $50k-$100k engagement as building durable organizational IP, not buying training sessions.
- **Brooker** — Skills extending to CRM and enterprise software is his vertical. Finance teams drowning in Salesforce and Bloomberg terminal workflows are the exact audience Santiago is describing.

**What Claudie already validates:**

Claudie runs 80+ skills today covering project management, email ghostwriting, sales proposals, dashboard updates, meeting follow-ups, presentation creation, pipeline ops, and weekly briefings. This is not a coding tool — it's an always-on chief of staff built entirely on the skill abstraction. The progressive 3-layer context loading (name/description at ~50 tokens, full instructions on-demand) is what makes this scale.

**How to use in client conversations:**

Santiago's framing solves a positioning problem. Clients hear "AI training" and think one-time workshop. "We build skills that encode your team's specific workflows" reframes the engagement as building organizational AI infrastructure. Every Consulting doesn't sell knowledge transfer — it builds the skill layer between your team and AI.

**Concrete next steps:**

1. Add "skill building" as an explicit deliverable line item in proposals — not just "custom prompts" but "production skills your team runs daily"
2. Build a 15-minute demo showing Claudie's skill system as proof-of-concept for enterprise clients
3. Have Brooker prototype one finance-vertical skill (e.g., portfolio review workflow) for non-tech sales conversations
4. Track platform risk: if skills become the standard abstraction, Anthropic may tighten or monetize the skill ecosystem
