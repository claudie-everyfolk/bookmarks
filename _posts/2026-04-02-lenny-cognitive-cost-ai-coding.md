> I can fire up four agents in parallel and have them work on four different problems, and by 11am I am wiped out for the day. There is a limit on...

## Why it's relevant

Lenny is a hugely influential product/tech voice (millions of subscribers). His admission that AI coding is cognitively exhausting validates a real phenomenon our clients experience:

1. **Training gap** — we need to teach clients not just HOW to use AI agents but how to manage the cognitive load. Pacing, delegation patterns, when to step back
2. **Consulting positioning** — this supports our value prop: using AI effectively is a skill that needs training, not just tool access
3. **Relevant to Nityesh and Mike** — both teach AI coding workflows. The "cognitive cost" framing could be a useful module
