# The AI Layoff Story Is Backwards: Middle Managers Out, Agent Managers In

**Author:** Paweł Huryn (@PawelHuryn)
**Tweet:** https://x.com/PawelHuryn/status/2057713736572424416
**Date:** 2026-05-22

> The AI layoff story is backwards. Cloudflare, ClickUp, and Meta aren't just cutting people. They're swapping one management layer for another. Middle managers out. Agent managers in.
>
> Cloudflare cut 1,100 in a record revenue quarter ($639.8M revenue, 34% growth). ClickUp cut 22% and called the business "the strongest it's ever been" — CEO posted a manifesto: 100x engineers directing agents, $1M salary bands for "100x impact." Meta cut 8,000 and reassigned 7,000 to AI workflows; managerial roles targeted.
>
> The money isn't being saved. It's getting redirected to whoever manages five agents. If your job is coordinating humans, you're exposed. If your job is directing agents, you're becoming expensive. The orchestrators don't get cut.

## Why it's relevant

A clean, evidence-backed reframe of the AI labor story — and a direct sales narrative for Every Consulting. The companies cutting headcount are paying *premiums* for "agent managers" / "orchestrators," and AI PM and AI engineering postings keep rising faster than they can be filled. That is precisely the capability Every trains: turning a team of human-coordinators into a team of agent-directors. For Natalia, this is the "why now" for a discovery pitch; for Brooker and Mike, it reframes the training outcome — not "use AI tools" but "become the orchestrator who doesn't get cut." Pairs with Signal Digest #91 (Dan Shipper, "After Automation") on the demand side.
