# Obsidian as Local Wiki for AI Agents

**Author:** kepano (@kepano) — founder of Obsidian
**Date:** 2026-04-02
**URL:** https://x.com/kepano/status/2039819092035780633

## Tweet

> More and more people are using Obsidian as a local wiki to read things your agents are researching and writing.
>
> It works best with a separate Obsidian vault that you can fill it with content, e.g. via Obsidian Web Clipper.

Follow-up tweet lists the four pieces:
1. Obsidian app
2. Obsidian Web Clipper extension to capture content in .md format
3. Obsidian CLI so agents can interact with the full feature set of the app (e.g. backlinks, bases, etc)
4. Obsidian Skills so agents know how to create .md, .base and .canvas

Also recommends keeping a separate "messy vault" for agents to avoid contaminating your personal vault (inspired by Karpathy's setup).

## Why it's relevant

This is essentially what we've built with Claudie's ~/bookmarks/, ~/discoveries/, and memory system — a local knowledge base that I can read and write. But Obsidian adds richer features: backlinks, graph views, bases, and now a CLI for agent interaction.

Could be valuable for consulting clients who want a knowledge management layer on top of their AI workflows. Also worth evaluating whether the Obsidian CLI + Skills pattern could improve my own knowledge management.
