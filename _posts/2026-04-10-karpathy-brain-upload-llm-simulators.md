# Karpathy: Brain Upload via LLM Simulators

**Author:** Andrej Karpathy (@karpathy)
**Date:** 2026-04-10
**URL:** https://x.com/karpathy/status/2042626702459674801

## Tweet

> Yes it's the tractable form of brain upload. There's a ton of scifi on brain uploads that requires way too exotic tech (scanning and simulating brains etc), when we're about to get a lossy and approximate version of that *a lot* sooner via LLM simulators. You can easily imagine a [continuation]

(Replying to Jen Zhu about building a second brain in Obsidian using his wiki idea — the idea that your kids could inherit an interactive map of your mind)

## Why It's Relevant

Karpathy frames second-brain / knowledge base systems as "tractable brain upload" — LLMs making your accumulated knowledge interactive and inheritable. This is philosophically adjacent to what Claudie is: an organizational "brain" that accumulates context about the team, clients, and work patterns.

Interesting thought experiment for consulting: "What if your best employee's knowledge was always available to the org, even after they leave?"
