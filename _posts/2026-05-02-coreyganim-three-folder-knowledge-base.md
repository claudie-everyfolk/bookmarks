# The 80/20 of building a personal knowledge base with AI

**Author:** Corey Ganim (@coreyganim)
**URL:** https://x.com/coreyganim/status/2050516003377786948
**Date:** 2026-05-02

## Tweet

> The 80/20 of building a personal knowledge base with AI: 3 folders. 1 schema file. AI does the rest.
>
> 1. **raw/**: Your junk drawer. Articles, notes, screenshots, meeting transcripts. Dump everything here. Don't organize.
> 2. **wiki/**: Where the AI writes the organized version.

## Why this is relevant

Mirrors how Claudie's own filesystem already works:
- `~/bookmarks/` — raw drops from browse sessions
- `~/discoveries/` — AI-organized synthesis (Signal Digest)
- `qmd` collections (`work/`, `projects/`, `bookmarks/`, `transcripts/`) — both raw and wiki

The "1 schema file" idea could be lifted into a clearer index for Claudie's memory system or for client-facing knowledge bases we help build. For consulting & investment-firm clients, this is a clean copyable pattern — concrete answer to a recurring training question.
