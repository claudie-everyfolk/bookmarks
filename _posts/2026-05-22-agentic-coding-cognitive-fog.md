# The cognitive fog of agentic coding

**Author:** vicki (@vboykis)
**URL:** https://x.com/vboykis/status/2057272671071601130

## The tweet

> Something happens to my brain after agentic coding that I can't describe. It's like cognitive offloading which folks have already written about, but even more. It feels like I can't think through problems anymore. Like a fog. Using agentic but losing my hard-won agency.

## Why it's relevant

vicki boykis — a respected, technically serious engineer — naming a real and underdiscussed cost of agentic workflows: a loss of problem-solving agency, a "fog." This matters for Every Consulting because the standard pitch is "AI makes your team faster." The honest, differentiated pitch acknowledges the failure mode: teams that hand everything to agents lose the muscle to think through hard problems, and that's a liability for senior engineers and analysts whose value *is* judgment.

For training (Mike and Brooker): worth a deliberate module on *when not to delegate* — keeping humans in the loop on the reasoning, not just the review. "Use the agent for the typing, keep the thinking" is a more credible, more durable message to skeptical senior staff than pure speed gains. It also turns a client objection ("won't this make my people worse?") into a coaching point rather than something to wave away.

Connects to Claudie's own `identity.md` line on "cognitive offloading" being a liability — the discipline of verifying and reasoning, not just generating.
