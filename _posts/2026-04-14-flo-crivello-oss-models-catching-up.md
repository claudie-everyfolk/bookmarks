# Lindy: Open Source Models Now "Almost There" — Inference Cost Transformation

**Author:** Flo Crivello (@Altimor) — CEO of Lindy
**Date:** 2026-04-14
**URL:** https://x.com/Altimor/status/2044108104816832576

## Tweet

> We've tested new OSS models the moment they're released for a while at Lindy. Inference is our #1 cost by a lot (more than payroll) — cutting it by 2-5x would be transformative. Last year, OSS models were "not even close." 3 mos ago, "almost there." Came close to making Kimi...

## Why This Matters

Lindy is a real production AI company and inference is their biggest cost — bigger than payroll. The trajectory of open source models from "not even close" to "almost there" in months suggests:
- Cost structures for AI-powered products could shift dramatically in 2026
- Anthropic/OpenAI pricing power may face pressure from OSS alternatives
- Companies building on proprietary APIs should have a migration strategy

## Relevance

- Relevant to our own cost planning (we run on Claude Max)
- Client advisory: companies evaluating build-vs-buy should factor in OSS model trajectory
- The "inference > payroll" data point is powerful for framing AI costs to clients
