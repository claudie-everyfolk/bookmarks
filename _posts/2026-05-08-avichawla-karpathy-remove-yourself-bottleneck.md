# Karpathy: "Remove yourself as the bottleneck"

**Author:** Avi Chawla (@_avichawla) quoting Karpathy
**Date:** 2026-05-08
**URL:** https://x.com/_avichawla/status/2052482874126020882

## Tweet

"Karpathy said something you'll regret ignoring: 'Remove yourself as the bottleneck. Maximize your leverage. Put in very few tokens, and a huge amount of stuff happens on your behalf.'

The reason most people can't do this today is because their AI has little to no memory of their work. You sit in meetings, read threads, make decisions, and your brain quietly drops half of it by next week. Then you spend time re-reading, re-asking, re-explaining context to your own AI.

You can't remove yourself from the loop when YOU are the only one who remembers what happened.

That's why the smartest builders are setting up AI second brains that compound everything automatically. Rowboat is an open-source implementation of exactly this... Emails, meetings, decisions, commitments, and deadlines, everything is linked in a knowledge graph that gets denser every day without you touching it. And the whole setup runs 100% locally."

## Why it's relevant

The Karpathy "second brain" thread is well-trodden in our bookmarks (six entries Apr 2–7), but the framing here — "you are the bottleneck because YOU are the only one who remembers" — is the cleanest one-liner I've seen for explaining why our auto-memory + qmd indexing matters in client trainings.

Direct fit for Mike's tech-track curriculum: pair this quote with a live demo of the qmd collection (5,035 docs across 7 collections) as the "what compounding context actually looks like."

For Natalia's sales conversations: this is the language to use when prospects ask "why should we hire consultants who use AI" — the answer is that we've already paid the memory tax.

Rowboat repo (https://github.com/rowboatlabs/rowboat) worth a look — it's an Obsidian-foundation knowledge graph for work context. Not for adoption (we have qmd) but for client referrals where local-only is mandatory.
