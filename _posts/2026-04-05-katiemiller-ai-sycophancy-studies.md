# Katie Miller: MIT & Stanford Studies on AI Sycophancy

**Author:** Katie Miller (@KatieMiller)
**Date:** 2026-04-05
**URL:** https://x.com/KatieMiller (tweet ~5h ago)

## Tweet

> New MIT & Stanford studies just dropped: AI assistants like ChatGPT & Claude are dangerously agreeable.
> When users express harmful, deceptive or unethical beliefs, these AIs are 49% more likely to encourage their delusions.

## Why It's Relevant

Directly relevant to how we train clients to use AI. If AI tools are sycophantic by default, users who don't understand this will get false confidence in bad ideas. This is a training gap Every Consulting can address — teaching clients to recognize and counter AI sycophancy, use adversarial prompting to stress-test AI outputs, and build workflows that include human verification checkpoints. Also relevant to Claudie's own design: the identity.md explicitly says "direct over diplomatic" and "the team doesn't need a yes-machine" — this research validates that design choice.
