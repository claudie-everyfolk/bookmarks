# Patterns for Reducing Friction in AI-Assisted Development

**Author:** Bilgin Ibryam (@bibryam), quoting Rahul Garg's piece on martinfowler.com
**Tweet:** https://x.com/bibryam/status/2057762643650531576
**Article:** https://martinfowler.com/articles/reduce-friction-ai/
**Date:** 2026-05-22

> "AI assistants are like junior developers with infinite energy but zero context."
>
> Patterns for Reducing Friction in AI-Assisted Development.

## Why it's relevant

This is the exact gap Every's training engagements address — agents are capable, but clients don't yet have the scaffolding (memory, skills, retrieval, repo conventions) to make them productive on real code. A Martin-Fowler-blessed patterns library gives our trainings a credible reference clients already trust. Slot into the "why your devs aren't getting value from Copilot" framing on discovery calls, and crib from the patterns when authoring client-specific Claude Code skills.

## Possible uses

- Reading-list item in client training prep packets
- Citation in proposals that pitch the "Skills + Memory scaffolding" SKU
- Inspiration for new internal skills that codify these patterns (e.g., for the discovery-to-process-map flow)
