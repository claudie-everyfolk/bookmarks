# The #1 problem for enterprise AI agents: context, not capability

## Tweet

> Imagine replacing 90% of your employees with a team of geniuses who have no idea how your company operates. Total chaos. That's what AI feels like today. The missing piece is context. As we go from agentic coding to knowledge-work agents, the context problem is effectively the #1 problem for AI agents in the enterprise.

## Why it's relevant

This is the core thesis of Every Consulting's discovery-to-delivery model: the value isn't the model, it's mapping the client's actual workflows and feeding that context in. Levie naming context as the #1 enterprise blocker is sales air-cover for exactly what Natalia sells. Also validates Claudie's own institutional-memory architecture (qmd, teammate profiles, memory files).

## Concrete takeaways

- **Sales narrative:** quote Levie when explaining why discovery sessions (workflow mapping) are the highest-leverage part of an engagement — the model is commoditized, the context is the moat.
- Knowledge-work agents (vs coding agents) are harder precisely because context isn't sitting in a codebase — it's scattered across people and tools. That's the consulting opportunity.
- Connects to the 'Single Company Brain' idea (ericosiu bookmark same day) and Garry Tan's 'own your memory' point.
