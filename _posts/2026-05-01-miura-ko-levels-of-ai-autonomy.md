# Most companies are still Level 1 — levels of AI autonomy framework

**Author:** Ann Miura-Ko (@annimaniac)
**Date:** 2026-05-01
**URL:** https://x.com/annimaniac/status/2050225284277026990

## Tweet (excerpt)

"Everyone wants to be AI-pilled. Most Companies Are Still Level 1.

Right now, 'AI-pilled' is being used as though it were binary. You either are or you aren't. In practice, companies differ both in intensity (how deeply AI is embedded into daily work across the organization) and in technical capability (what AI is actually allowed to see, do, and change).

A company where employees use ChatGPT to summarize meetings is not in the same category as a company where agents can query systems of record, take bounded action, propagate workflows across teams, and improve the way future work gets done. Both may describe themselves as AI-forward. They are not operating at the same level.

The better question is: What level of autonomy has the organization actually achieved?
- What can AI see? Is the work of your company legible to a machine, or does it live in someone's mind?
- What can AI do? Can it act on systems of record, or only summarize what humans already wrote down?
- Who can extend the system? Are non-engineers shipping production internal tools?
- How has the organization changed? Or are you running 2023's org chart with better autocomplete?"

## Why it's relevant

This is **directly the framework Every Consulting sells against**. Natalia's discovery sessions effectively assess "what level is this client at?" — Bloomberg, NYT, hedge funds all sit somewhere on this ladder. Miura-Ko just gave the framework a clean public articulation tied to AV-style autonomy levels.

**Concrete uses:**
- Sales/discovery: ask the four questions above as part of every initial call. Output a "Level X" snapshot in the discovery deck.
- Training narrative: the Every differentiator is moving clients past Level 1 (chat) toward Level 2-3 (agents acting on systems of record). Mike teaches the tooling; Brooker teaches the workflows. This framing names exactly what they do.
- Proposal language: "we will move you from Level 1 to Level 2 in workflow X" is sharper than "we will help you adopt AI."

Worth flagging to Natalia — could become house language for the consulting practice.
