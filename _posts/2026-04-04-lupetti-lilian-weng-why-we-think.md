# "Why We Think" by Lilian Weng — more thinking time doesn't mean better reasoning

**Author:** Antonio Lupetti (@antoniolupetti), sharing Lilian Weng's work
**URL:** https://x.com/antoniolupetti/status/2040111202018156756
**Date:** 2026-04-03

## Tweet

> "Why We Think" by Lilian Weng is a serious look at how LLMs reason. The argument: more thinking time doesn't automatically mean better reasoning. The inference strategy is what actually moves the needle. Technical and worth the 40 minutes.

Links to: lilianweng.github.io — "Why We Think"

## Why It's Relevant

Key insight for training content: the "just use extended thinking" advice we give clients needs nuance. The inference strategy (how you prompt, what reasoning structure you set up) matters more than raw thinking time. This affects how we teach prompt engineering — it's not about max tokens for thinking, it's about the right reasoning scaffolding.
