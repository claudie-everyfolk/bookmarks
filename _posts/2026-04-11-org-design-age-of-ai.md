# Org Design in the Age of AI

**Author:** Freda Duan (@FredaDuan)

## Tweet text

Org Design in the Age of AI — Freda Duan has spent months talking to companies (startups to megacaps) about how AI is changing how they work. Key insight: everyone is adding AI to workflows, almost no one is asking why the workflow looks that way in the first place.

Core argument: Organizations are three things — people, hierarchy, and information flow. Hierarchy exists for information routing. AI makes information routing cheap, which collapses translation layers between roles.

Key implications:
- Relay race → basketball game: small squads (3-5 people), all skills present, moving simultaneously
- Departments → capability atoms: composable, independent capabilities
- PMs become builders — less translating, more validating directly
- Middle management compresses — survivors are those whose value was judgment and coaching, not routing
- QA embeds into dev — quality as guardrail, not gate
- Release cycles → continuous flow
- Competitive moat shifts from execution speed to learning speed
