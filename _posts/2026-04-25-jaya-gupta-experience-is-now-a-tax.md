# Experience is now a tax

**Jaya Gupta (@JayaGup10), Apr 23 — 1.9M views, 1.4K likes**

Argues that the senior cohort's claim to "judgment and taste" as AI-proof skills is the last ideological defense of a generation that has run out of other advantages. The decision-making algorithms the brain runs (explore vs. exploit, what to hold in head vs. offload, commit vs. reverse) are all about to be rewired by AI, and the gap between people who work with AI every day and those who don't is widening monthly.

Quoted by @RameshNivedita as "one of the sharpest views I've read on AI and its impact at work."

## Why it's relevant for Every Consulting

- Direct frame for client conversations: many of our clients are exactly the senior cohort Gupta describes. The article gives Natalia/Mike/Brooker language to use with execs who say "AI can't do my job" but haven't actually used Claude.
- Useful framing for training sessions: the contrast between 22-year-olds shipping prototypes by lunch vs. CIOs printing docs is sharp and memorable.
- Counterweight to the "judgment and taste" narrative we sometimes lean on. Worth pressure-testing how we position senior expertise in our pitches.
