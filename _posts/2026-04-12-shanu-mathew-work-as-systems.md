# "The Big Unlock: Seeing Work as Systems"

**Author:** Shanu Mathew (@ShanuMathew93)
**URL:** https://x.com/ShanuMathew93/status/2043256476093820979
**Date:** 2026-04-12

## Tweet

The big unlock: stop thinking of code as a technical skill and start thinking of it as a way of seeing work. Every workflow becomes a question of inputs, logic, data, and outputs. Every repeatable task becomes a candidate for automation.

Most companies sit on massive backlogs of work that never got automated — engineering was too scarce, ROI wasn't worth it, too much unstructured data. AI changes the calculus on all of those simultaneously. As the cost of building and intelligence keeps falling, more projects clear the hurdle.

## Why it's relevant

This is essentially Every Consulting's pitch distilled into a tweet thread. The "work as systems" framing is exactly what our discovery sessions do — help clients see their workflows as decomposable, automatable systems. The point about massive backlogs of unautomated work is the demand story we sell.

Good reference material for Natalia's sales conversations and Brooker's non-tech training — this is the mindset shift we're trying to create in every engagement.
