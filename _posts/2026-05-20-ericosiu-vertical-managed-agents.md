# Customization + onboarding is the gap between a 9/10 and a 7/10 agent

**Author:** Eric Siu (@ericosiu)
**URL:** https://x.com/ericosiu/status/2057107268563509637
**Date:** 2026-05-20

> We've been setting up individual agents for our team and one thing is clear: the more time we spend customizing the agent and onboarding the team member, the more success they have with it.
>
> Someone who rates it a 9/10 in terms of usefulness has it doing data pulls, strategizing, and executing end to end.
>
> Someone who rates it a 7/10 is missing key connectors and is more likely to give up and do something else because they're just too busy.
>
> So what does this mean? It means that managed agents will have huge demand. All the companies building horizontal single brains to chat with will realize at some point that there is good money to be made with vertical managed agents.

## Why it's relevant

A practitioner's empirical confirmation of Every Consulting's core thesis: adoption success is a function of customization depth and per-person onboarding, not tooling. The 9/10-vs-7/10 split — and the "too busy, gave up" failure mode — is exactly the adoption-dropoff Every sees in client engagements. The "vertical managed agents" prediction maps directly onto Every's underused retainer revenue.

- **Adoption dropoff is real and on file.** Bloomberg kickoff transcript: fall pilot ran 150 participants, only 50 finished. Engineers Gate kickoff: "other functions too busy with day-to-day work for AI upskilling." Exactly Siu's 7/10 "too busy, gave up" persona.

### Concrete actions
- **Sales positioning:** add a "9/10 vs 7/10" framing to proposals — sell onboarding depth and connector setup, not session count.
- **Productize the retainer:** reframe the AI Champion Retainer / monthly tiers as "Managed Agent" — connector setup, prompt maintenance, ongoing tuning. The structure exists; it needs the name and the pitch.
- **Training deliverables:** make "connectors wired + per-person onboarding" an explicit, billed line item — transcripts show that's where adoption breaks.

### Tension / confirmation
Mostly confirmation of Every's discovery → custom-prompts → support model. One useful tension: Siu frames managed agents as a recurring *service*, while Every's pricing still weights toward one-time project fees ($50k–$100k).
