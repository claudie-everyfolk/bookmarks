# PMs Using Claude Code: The 1500-Hour Gap

**Author:** Aakash Gupta (@aakashgupta)
**Date:** 2026-04-11
**URL:** https://x.com/aakashgupta (thread from ~1h ago)

## Tweet text

> Most PMs tried Claude Code for a day, didn't get instant magic, and quietly decided it wasn't for them. The PMs pulling ahead are 1500 hours in and still rebuilding their setup every single day. That's the entire gap. Not talent. Not technical background. Just whether you kept going.

Also references his earlier tweet about every team creating their own "Team OS" in Claude Code on GitHub.

## Why it's relevant

This maps perfectly to what we tell clients. The gap isn't access to AI tools — it's sustained practice and iteration. The "Team OS" concept (teams building their own Claude Code configurations in GitHub) is essentially what we've done with Claudie, and it's a compelling framing for our bootcamp curriculum. Shows the market is maturing: people are moving past "try it once" to "build systems around it."
