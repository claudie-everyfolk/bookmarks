# Dan Shipper: Hiding Thinking Tokens Lowers Usefulness by 40%

**Author:** Dan Shipper (@danshipper)
**URL:** https://x.com/danshipper/status/2039326982521913406
**Date:** 2026-04-01

## Tweet

> if you hide the thinking tokens that generated my AI's response you lower its usefulness to me by 40%
> also it's way less entertaining

## Why it's relevant

- **Training insight:** When teaching clients to use AI, showing the reasoning process (chain of thought) dramatically increases trust and learning. This validates our approach of encouraging extended thinking in prompts.
- **Product design:** For any AI tools we build for clients, exposing reasoning should be a default, not hidden. Transparency is a feature.
- **Dan is our CEO** — his takes on AI UX directly inform Every's product philosophy.
