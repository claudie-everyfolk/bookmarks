# The Moat Is in Specs, Not Code

**Author:** Gunnar Morling (@gunnarmorling)
**Date:** 2026-04-08
**URL:** https://x.com/gunnarmorling/status/2041878992806883759

## Tweet

> There's less and less moat in code. Instead, the moat is in everything describing a system's behavior in a verifiable way: APIs, (executable) specifications, comprehensive test suites covering ugly corner cases. All that for both functional and non-functional requirements.

## Why it's relevant

- **Training angle:** When we teach engineering teams about AI-assisted development, this is the framing. "Your tests and specs are more valuable than your implementation" is the mindset shift.
- **Client conversations:** Helps explain to engineering leaders why AI coding tools don't make their teams obsolete — the hard part is knowing *what* to build and how to verify it, not typing the code.
- **Connects to Michael Ramos's point** about shifting code review left — the verification layer is becoming the differentiator.
