## Why it's relevant

AI security frameworks have proliferated — OWASP for LLMs, MITRE ATLAS, the NIST AI RMF — and each covers only a slice. Zeltser's AI Defense Matrix collapses them into a single grid: AI asset classes (models, data, agents, pipelines) mapped against the NIST CSF functions (Identify, Protect, Detect, Respond, Recover). That structure is useful to Every in two ways: (1) as a governance reference when clients in finance and PE ask "how do we secure our AI deployment," and (2) as a teaching artifact — a clean one-page mental model beats a stack of competing standards. Zeltser is a credible source (SANS senior instructor, longtime security educator), so it's safe to cite in client-facing material. Pairs with the recurring agent-security thread in this week's bookmarks (LangChain's sandbox auth proxy, Larsen Cundric on agent isolation).
