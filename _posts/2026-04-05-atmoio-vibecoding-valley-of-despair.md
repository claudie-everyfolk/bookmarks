# Mo Bitar: The Vibecoding Valley of Despair

**Author:** Mo Bitar (@atmoio)
**Date:** 2026-04-05
**URL:** https://x.com/atmoio/status/2040897459564490996

## Tweet

> there's a sort of permanent valley of despair i'm seeing a lot of non-technical people who are building software fall into.
> they have a wonderful time vibecoding the thing, but when they reach the point where everything is functionally correct, they have no idea what to do next.

## Why It's Relevant

This is the exact gap Every Consulting fills for non-technical clients. Brooker's vertical — ChatGPT for operations, AI for analysis — serves exactly these people. They can vibecode something that works, but they hit a wall on deployment, maintenance, debugging, and iteration. This tweet frames a real pain point that could inform how we position training: not just "learn to use AI" but "learn to go from prototype to production." Could shape bootcamp curriculum and discovery session questions.
