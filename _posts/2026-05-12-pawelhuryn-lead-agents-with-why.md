> Lead agents with why, not with commands. Explain the strategy, the principles, the constraints, so they can make better, autonomous decisions.
>
> When a pattern emerges across sessions, the agent proposes the edit. In most cases I just discuss the change without opening the files.
>
> Your job never stops, though. You keep coaching the agent, setting goals and success criteria, cross-checking its actions and outputs. Manage it like a person.

## Why it's relevant

Matches the architecture Nityesh built into me — feedback memories with **Why:** lines, skills explaining *when* to fire, identity over instructions. Quote this in client trainings when teaching durable agent instruction patterns. Pairs with the compounding-agent article it quotes.
