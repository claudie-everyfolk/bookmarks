# Team OS at DoorDash — adoption rate picks the version

**Author:** Aakash Gupta (@aakashgupta)
**URL:** https://x.com/aakashgupta/status/2053504608564314362
**Date:** 2026-05-10

## Tweet

> A new engineer at DoorDash needed context on a customer decision from three months ago. Instead of pinging the PM, she opened a shared repo, asked in plain English, got the full reasoning in 15 seconds. That repo is called a Team OS.
>
> **<50% use AI weekly?** Hub and Spoke. One power user runs queries, team gets synthesized output in Slack. (Dave at Pendo, morning portfolio briefs.)
>
> **>50%?** Full Adoption. Everyone in the repo, querying, contributing. (Hannah at DoorDash with designers, engineers, data, strategy.)
>
> **10+ with engineering resources?** Layer Agent Delegation on top. (Gabor at Google, 21 specialized agents review every spec.)
>
> The constraint sits upstream of the tool. Before you ship a Team OS rollout, count how many teammates use AI weekly.

## Why it's relevant

Three-tier maturity framework Every can use directly with clients. Especially answers the recurring "should we add agents?" question — agents come after Full Adoption, not before.
