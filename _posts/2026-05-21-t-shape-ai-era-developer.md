# The T-shape for thriving as an AI-era developer (Google I/O)

**Author:** Zara Zhang (@zarazhangrui)
**Tweet:** https://x.com/zarazhangrui/status/2057267931025957348

> Great slide from the "How to thrive as an AI-era developer" session at Google I/O today. I think this T-shape will apply to not just developers but every job function. We need to: go deeper with our domain expertise, go wider with adjacent skills and fields, learn to use AI.

## Why it's relevant

A clean, slide-ready framework for Every's training narrative: the AI-era professional needs depth (domain expertise), breadth (adjacent skills), and AI fluency. It generalizes beyond developers to every job function — which is exactly Every's pitch to non-technical client teams. A simple mental model like this is gold for opening a training session or a discovery deck. It dovetails with the "After Automation" thesis (deep experts become more valuable, not less) and is directly usable by Mike (developer audiences) and Brooker (operations/finance audiences) as a framing slide.
