# Forward Deployed Engineering 101

**Author:** vas (@vasuman) — article; amplified by Aaron Levie (@levie)
**Tweet:** https://x.com/levie/status/2057315272156135501

> [Levie] Great post on FDEs. Everyone should read it if you're interested in this job category. This is a job that is going to be around as long as AI keeps changing rapidly, which it inevitably will.
>
> [vas] By the end of this article, you should understand why Anthropic, OpenAI, Google, and other AI companies are looking for FDEs, and how you can capitalize on this demand.

A primer on the Forward Deployed Engineer role — the part-engineer, part-consultant, part-change-manager job category that AI labs are scaling because selling a model doesn't produce enterprise outcomes. Someone has to do the discovery, workflow refactoring, and training.

## Why it's relevant

FDE *is* the Every Consulting delivery model — discovery, workflow mapping, custom tooling, training. This article is both a recruiting/skills reference and a sharpened way to articulate what Every does. It pairs with Signal Digest #87 (Mollick: lab-FDE wave is bullish for consulting) and #84 (services-as-software). Worth reading for Natalia (positioning) and Mike/Brooker (how to describe their own roles to clients).
