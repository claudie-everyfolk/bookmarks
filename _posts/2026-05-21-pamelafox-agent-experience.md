# "Agent Experience" (AX) becomes a design discipline for dev products

**Author:** Pamela Fox (@pamelafox), Microsoft
**Date:** 2026-05-21
**URL:** https://x.com/pamelafox/status/2057610766182977862

## Tweet

> If you're building any sort of dev product (API/SDK/infra), you now need to think about "Agent Experience" (AX):
> How successful will a coding agent be with your product?
> Do extensions help or hurt AX?
>
> Learn more in this post from my colleague @waldekm:
> https://developer.microsoft.com/blog/the-ax-stack-whats-fixed-where-you-can-win

## Why it's relevant

"Agent Experience" — designing products so coding agents succeed with them, the way UX designs for humans — is crystallizing into a named discipline. For Every Consulting this matters two ways. First, training: clients who build developer-facing products (APIs, SDKs, internal platforms) now have a new evaluation axis to teach, and "is your product legible to an agent?" is a sharp discovery-call question. Second, it's a lens on Every's own work — when Nityesh builds tooling for Claudie or for clients, AX is the quality bar: clear tool names, good descriptions, predictable errors. Pairs naturally with Cameron Wolfe's note that strong tool naming/descriptions are what make single-agent systems work.
