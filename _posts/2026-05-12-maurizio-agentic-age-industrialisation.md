> We were all convinced GenAI was a magic trick, but I think the honeymoon is finally ending.
>
> [...] As AI gets "smarter", the work of implementing it is getting *significantly* more technical. [...] we actually don't need fewer engineers, we need an ARMY of them to build the digital plumbing (eg, the CLIs and Model Context Protocols) that allows an agent to talk to a 20-year-old+ legacy database.
>
> Compute is being treated like a raw utility [...] the "all-in-one" platform appears under threat because agents do not care about a slick UI, they need to talk to the guts of a system.
>
> Enterprises are starting to treat headless capability as a deal-breaker.

## Why it's relevant

Confirms the thesis we already sell: enterprises need engineering work to make AI useful, not just licenses. Useful for tech-vertical decks (Mike) and a "why MCPs matter" explainer. Reinforces the case for custom client tooling work and retainer offerings.
