# Riley Brown: The Future Is Persistent Agents, Not Chat UIs

- **Author:** Riley Brown (@rileybrown)
- **URL:** https://x.com/rileybrown/status/2043240946578706732
- **Date:** 2026-04-12

## Tweet

> Personally I don't see the point of Cowork. I don't even see the point of chatgpt or Claude anymore.
>
> I either want to talk to a claw (agent with all my skills running on a persistent computer with NO guardrails in iMessage or telegram) or i want to use Claude Code or Codex app

Quote tweet: "Codex App > Claude Desktop App"

## Why it's relevant

This is literally describing Claudie's architecture — a persistent agent on a dedicated machine with skills, running autonomously. The framing of "persistent computer with all my skills" vs "chat UI" validates the direction Every Consulting took. The mention of iMessage/Telegram as interface channels (vs Slack for us) shows the pattern is emerging independently across builders. The "NO guardrails" part is where we diverge — we have ring-based access control, which is the right call for a business context.
