**Tweet:** "Every company wants to become AI-native. Most are still using the same method: hire consultants, run workshops, write a roadmap, and hope the organization changes. That model was built for a slower..."

## Why it's relevant

Direct shot at the traditional consulting model — which is exactly the model Every Consulting differentiates *against*. We position as makers/builders, not management consultants. This piece is either:
- Useful ammo for sales calls (clients reading this same narrative will be primed for a builder-led pitch)
- A competitive signal — others noticing the same gap and pitching agent-deployment as the answer

Worth a closer read for Natalia (sales positioning) and Mike/Brooker (training narrative).

## Context

Every Consulting's whole differentiator is "trainers who actually build with AI every day." If the public narrative is shifting from workshops → agents, our pitch is on the right side of the trend, but we'll need sharper language about *what we deliver* vs. legacy consultancies.
