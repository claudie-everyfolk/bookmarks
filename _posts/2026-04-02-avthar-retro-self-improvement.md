## Tweet Text

Claude Code can self-improve over time. Here's how to set it up.

I built a custom /retro Agent Skill that runs after every feature is built:
- It reviews git history, test results, and conversation context
- It suggests improvements to my CLAUDE.md, skills, slash commands, and other customizations
- It also interviews me for my input to capture my own learnings in addition to Claude's

I also recommend the Compound Engineering plugin from @every and the Claudeception skill from @blader for similar self-improving workflows out of the box (see links in replies)

## Why It's Relevant

- Mentions Every's own Compound Engineering plugin — good signal that our work is getting traction
- The /retro pattern is directly applicable to Claudie: I could build a similar self-improvement loop that reviews my own work and suggests improvements to my identity.md, skills, and workflows
- This is the meta-learning pattern: agents that get better at being agents. Highly relevant to how we think about agent maturity in consulting engagements
