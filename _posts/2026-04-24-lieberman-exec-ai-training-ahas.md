# Lieberman's 7 aha moments from exec AI training at a major bank

Alex Lieberman co-led an AI training for 20 execs at one of the world's largest financial institutions and wrote up the biggest aha moments.

## Tweet

Just co-led an AI training for 20 execs from one of the largest financial institutions in the world. We covered a lot of ground from how LLMs work to what an agent is and how to transform your work, but here were the biggest aha moments for the group.

1. **Token budget as a new line item.** Historically leaders think about headcount and software budgets. Now token budget is a serious consideration — limits per employee, measuring ROI, allocation, and teaching employees which models to pick.

2. **Most bad output is user error, not model error.** The biggest contributor is bad context management — understanding the context window, separation of concerns, and treating context as a precious resource.

3. **"Skill" is just an SOP.** A one-page step-by-step doc for how to build a great deck is a skill. A skill is a long prompt that codifies a repeatable process, can be edited, and generates predictable output.

4. **Process mapping is the real unlock.** Saying "we use AI" gets you style points — the real move is mapping a team process step by step and marking each step E (eliminate), A (automate), or D (delegate). Surfaces AI opportunities AND undetected inefficiencies.

5. **Claude Cowork is the layperson's gateway drug to agents.** Most execs still think AI = supercharged Google chat. Cowork unlocks the "oh, I can build web apps, workflows, and artifacts in one place" moment.

6. **Flip the script on legal/compliance/IT.** The "bad guys" slowing transformation become heroes when leadership partners with them on calculated bets, frames the risk of doing nothing, and celebrates them publicly.

7. **Four things must go right for AI systems to perform:** right model, right process, right context, right guardrails. People over-weight model choice and under-invest in context and guardrails.

## Why this matters for Every Consulting

This is a near-perfect articulation of the Every Consulting training thesis. Mike, Brooker, and Natalia run the same kind of exec sessions — many of these aha moments (process mapping with E/A/D, skills-as-SOPs, context hygiene, Cowork as gateway drug) are already in their decks and talk tracks. Strong validation that the curriculum is pointed at the right instincts.

Specific reuses:
- **The E/A/D framing** is cleaner than our current "what can AI do here" prompt — we should test it as a live exercise in the next training.
- **Token budget as exec conversation** is a frame we don't use yet. Worth adding as a leadership-tier talking point (especially for CFO/COO-type attendees).
- **"Skill is just an SOP"** is the simplest definition I've seen — drop this into the opening module for any training deck. Removes the scary-word friction.
- **"Flip the script on legal/compliance"** — we hear this objection constantly; Lieberman's framing (celebrate them publicly when they ship) is a crisp reply Mike/Brooker could use in the wild.

Also useful as third-party social proof: another practitioner running the same workshops at the same caliber of client is independently landing on the same conclusions.
