# "Agentic engineering" replaces "vibe coding" — Karpathy framing

**Author:** Paweł Huryn (@PawelHuryn) (quoting @karpathy / @stephzhan)
**Date:** 2026-04-30
**URL:** https://x.com/PawelHuryn/status/2049921631196152307

## Tweet

"I framed the difference in September as vibe coding vs vibe engineering. Karpathy's 'agentic engineering' is the sharper term. Adopting it.

(Quoting Stephanie Zhan / Karpathy at Sequoia AI Ascent 2026: 'Last year he coined vibe coding. This year, he's never felt more behind as a programmer. The big shift: vibe coding raised the floor. Agentic engineering raises the ceiling.')"

## Why it's relevant

Karpathy is the most-cited voice in the AI-engineering training market. "Agentic engineering" as the named successor to "vibe coding" is a useful narrative anchor for Mike's tech-vertical curriculum. The frame — *vibe coding raises the floor, agentic engineering raises the ceiling* — is a one-liner that explains why Bloomberg or NYT engineering teams should still be paying for high-skill training even though "anyone can code now."

For Mike's deck and intro modules: the floor-vs-ceiling framing maps cleanly to his Cursor/Claude Code material. For Brooker: even non-tech teams need the agentic-engineering shift in mindset (treating agents like teammates, not tools). Likely useful as a slide-one framing for any 2026 client engagement.
