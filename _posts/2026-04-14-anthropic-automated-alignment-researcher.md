# Anthropic: Automated Alignment Researcher (Opus 4.6 closes 97% gap)

**Author:** Anthropic (@AnthropicAI)
**Date:** 2026-04-14
**URL:** https://x.com/AnthropicAI/status/2044138481790648323

## Tweet Thread

> New Anthropic Fellows research: developing an Automated Alignment Researcher.
>
> We ran an experiment to learn whether Claude Opus 4.6 could accelerate research on a key alignment problem: using a weak AI model to supervise the training of a stronger one.
>
> Here, we measure success by the fraction of the "performance gap" we can close between the weak model and the potential of the strong model. After 7 days, human researchers closed it by 23%. Then, our Automated Alignment Researchers—Opus 4.6 with extra tools—closed it by 97%.
>
> The AARs' best-performing method successfully generalized to both coding and math tasks, though their second-best method only generalized to math.

## Why This Is Interesting

This is a landmark result in AI safety research automation. Opus 4.6 acting as an alignment researcher outperformed human researchers by 4x on the weak-to-strong supervision problem. Key implications:

1. **AI safety research can be accelerated by AI itself** — this is the alignment community's best-case scenario playing out
2. **Weak-to-strong supervision is the core problem** for training future, more capable models — progress here matters enormously
3. **The generalization result is mixed** — best method worked on coding + math, second-best only math. This nuance matters.
4. **Validates the "Opus as researcher" paradigm** — same model family we run on

## Relevance to Every Consulting

- Direct evidence that frontier models can do sophisticated research tasks, not just execute instructions
- Useful talking point with clients wondering about AI capability ceiling
- Reinforces that the harness/tooling around the model (what Anthropic gave the AARs) is what unlocks capability — core Every thesis
