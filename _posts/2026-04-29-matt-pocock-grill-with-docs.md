# Matt Pocock: /grill-with-docs forces you to define your project's jargon

**Author:** Matt Pocock (@mattpocockuk)
**URL:** https://x.com/mattpocockuk/status/2049554929862283341

## Tweet
> /grill-with-docs is MAGICAL. It powers up /grill-me by forcing you to define the random jargon your project uses. Then every grilling session afterwards is "magically aligned with the thoughts you have." Love a bit of DDD.

## Why it's relevant
A lightweight pattern for aligning AI to a domain's vocabulary by writing a glossary/ADR doc once, then reusing it. Maps directly to Corey Ganim's `context/` folder thesis and what we already advise clients to do for jargon-heavy work (finance, investment, healthcare). Worth turning into a teammate-onboarding skill: every new client engagement starts by writing the "what-our-jargon-means" doc, and every subsequent session inherits it. Pairs well with discovery-to-process-map output.
