# Own your agent harness

**Author:** Viv (@Vtrivedy10)
**Date:** 2026-04-29
**URL:** https://x.com/Vtrivedy10/status/2049597811226726682

## Tweet

"own your agent harness, own your intelligence, own your evals — open ecosystems. this doesn't mean start from scratch, build on an open base harness where you can edit/extend existing primitives like bash, fs-ops, permissions, skills support, etc for your task harnesses." (QT of @jobergum: "Don't outsource your agent harness")

## Why it's relevant

Reinforces Signal Digest #75 (harness engineering as a discipline). The "own your harness" framing is now a recurring theme — Cursor SDK, Claude Agent SDK, Tony Gentilcore, elvis/DAIR, and now Bergum/Vtrivedy all hit it the same week. Direct training-narrative implication for Every: clients should not just consume Claude Code; they should own a base harness they edit and extend (bash, fs-ops, permissions, skills, hooks). That's exactly what Nityesh has built for the team.
