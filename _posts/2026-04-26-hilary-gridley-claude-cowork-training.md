# "Meeting non-technical people where they are" radically limits AI training value

> my problem with Claude Cowork is the same problem I have with most AI trainings: "meeting non-technical people where they are" radically limits what they can get out of AI. the technology can be genuinely transformational for people who have never touched or thought about code

24K views, 253 likes. Tweet from Apr 25.

## Why it's relevant
Direct critique of the dominant AI training paradigm Every Consulting operates in. Hilary's argument: trainings that stay safely inside non-technical comfort zones leave the biggest unlock — code, agents, automation — on the table. For a consulting practice running role-based hands-on workshops (Mike, Brooker, Nityesh, Natalia all deliver these), this is a sharp prompt: are we under-shipping the transformative parts because we're calibrating to "where they are"?

Worth flagging to Natalia / Nityesh as a positioning question for the next training cohort: how aggressive should the technical stretch be?
