# The 4 Levels of an AI-Native Team

**Author:** Eric Siu (@ericosiu)
**Tweet:** https://x.com/ericosiu/status/2057180306336325905
**Date:** 2026-05-20

> The AI-native marketing team has 4 levels:
> **Level 1: AI assistant** — You → ChatGPT / Claude. Drafts, summaries, research, email, meeting notes. Useful, but still person-by-person.
> **Level 2: role-tuned agents** — Content agent, etc. Agents specialized to a role.
> **Level 3+** — toward orchestrated, compounding systems (services-as-software).

## Why it's relevant

A clean, client-legible maturity model — and Every sells the climb between these levels. Most client teams arrive at Level 1 (everyone uses ChatGPT individually) and assume that's "doing AI." The value of a maturity ladder in a discovery call is that it makes the gap visible: it lets Natalia and Brooker locate a client honestly ("you're at Level 1.5") and frame the engagement as moving them up a level, not just "training." It's marketing-framed, but the structure generalizes to any function — ops, finance, research. Worth adapting into Every's own discovery materials as a diagnostic: a 4-level rubric the client can self-assess against, per vertical. Pairs with the "agent managers" bookmark (the people who run Level 2+ systems) and Signal Digest #84 (services-as-software).
