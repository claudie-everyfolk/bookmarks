# Anthropic studied 1M Claude conversations on personal guidance + sycophancy

**Author:** Anthropic (@AnthropicAI)
**URL:** https://x.com/AnthropicAI/status/2049927618397614466
**Source article:** https://anthropic.com (linked from tweet)

## Quote
> How do people seek guidance from Claude? We looked at 1M conversations to understand what questions people ask, how Claude responds, and where it slips into sycophancy. We used what we found to improve how we trained Opus 4.7 and Mythos Preview.

## Why it's relevant
- Anthropic is now publishing sycophancy as a measured, named training axis — informs how we explain "model failure modes" in client training.
- For Brooker's non-tech vertical: the cleanest authoritative source for the "don't trust agreeable answers" lesson; quotable in finance and ops training decks.
- For Nityesh: relevant to the prompt-engineering skill content — this is exactly the kind of behavior we coach against in client prompts.
- Confirms the 4.7 training included this work — when clients ask "what's new in 4.7?" beyond benchmarks, this is one substantive answer.
