# Enterprise AI Adoption: The Superuser Gap and Permission Problem

**Author:** Taylor Johnson (@taylor_jrj2012)
**Date:** 2026-04-12
**URL:** https://x.com/taylor_jrj2012/status/2043034472459977214

## Tweet Text

I've been thinking about enterprise AI adoption wrong.

My assumption was always: get the right tooling in place, show people what's possible, incentivize usage. Adoption follows.

That assumes a level of agency most employees don't have. (They don't.)

Saw this firsthand at my own company. You can hand someone Claude and a workflow and they'll still wait for permission to use it. The superuser gap isn't just about skill. It's about willingness to act without a playbook.

The vast majority of enterprises are going to have AI deployment partners that do the heavy lifting. Workflows, internal tooling, agents, the whole stack. They're the dev partners that build and deploy.

And here's the real unlock for execs: you can blame the partner if it goes wrong and take credit if it goes right. That's not a bug. That's the entire buying psychology of enterprise services.

## Why This Matters

This is a near-perfect description of Every Consulting's value proposition:
- "People wait for permission to use it" — this is exactly what we see in training sessions. Adoption isn't a tooling problem, it's a permission problem.
- "AI deployment partners that do the heavy lifting" — that's us. We build the workflows, prompts, and agents.
- "Blame the partner / take credit" — this is the enterprise buying psychology Natalia navigates in every sales conversation.
- The "superuser gap" framing could be useful in proposals — it's not about making everyone an expert, it's about creating enough permission structures and playbooks that normal employees feel safe using AI.
