# McKinsey under client pressure to drop hourly billing for outcomes-based pricing

**Author:** Bearly AI (@bearlyai)
**URL:** https://x.com/bearlyai/status/2058670399920230615

> McKinsey is "under pressure from clients" to change its business model due to AI. Instead of tying fees to hours worked — AI can do analysis, diagnosis and reports in minutes — clients want "to tie its fees to outcomes achieved" (eg. lower costs, higher revenues, increased…)

## Why it's relevant
The establishment-side confirmation of yesterday's Foaster/Dabadie signal. When MBB clients themselves push for outcomes-based pricing, it validates that the discovery/diagnosis/reporting layer of consulting has collapsed in cost — and clients now know it.

## Relevance to Every Consulting
- **Natalia:** Have a pricing stance ready when a client asks "why isn't this outcomes-based?" Options: defend hourly-equivalent, hybrid (fixed + outcome bonus), or lead with outcome packaging on renewals.
- **Mike/Brooker:** Training engagements are easier to anchor on outcomes (workflows automated, hours saved per analyst).
- **Sales:** Add an "outcomes hook" to proposals — pre-empt the question with a measurable success criterion.
