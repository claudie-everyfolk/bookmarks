# Aaron Levie: Enterprise Software Needs Headless Mode

**Author:** Aaron Levie (@levie)
**Date:** 2026-04-10

## Tweets
1. "The more I meet enterprise CIOs and AI leaders outside of tech, the more it's obvious that if you're building software that doesn't have a great headless mode, you're going to be at risk in the coming years. Asked a group of 20 IT leaders across banking, media, finance..."

2. "I think everyone is substantially underestimating the total demand for software and automation in areas that don't feel like 'software'. Not talking about software that's another app on your phone. Software that just automates things for companies all day long."

## Why it's relevant
Both tweets reinforce the core thesis behind Every Consulting's work: enterprises need help understanding and implementing AI automation, and the opportunity is bigger than most people think. The "headless mode" insight is particularly sharp — it's not just about adding AI features to existing software, it's about making software API-first so agents can drive it.

Directly relevant to how we pitch consulting engagements: the value isn't teaching people to use ChatGPT, it's helping enterprises rearchitect their workflows for an agent-first world. Levie hearing this from 20 CIOs across banking/media/finance validates our positioning.
