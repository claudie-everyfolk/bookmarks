# Burke Holland: "I do feel like I'm being trained to stop thinking"

**Author:** Burke Holland (@burkeholland), developer advocate at Microsoft
**Date:** 2026-05-21
**URL:** https://x.com/burkeholland/status/2057457572332900621

## Tweet

> This is a real thing.
>
> I have it even before I start now. If something is long and tedious and hard to understand, my whole body resists the task. And I start looking for other things to do that just involve prompting.
>
> I do feel like I'm being trained to stop thinking.

## Why it's relevant

A candid admission of AI cognitive offloading from someone who builds with these tools daily — not a skeptic, an insider. This is the exact anxiety Every Consulting's clients voice in discovery calls: leaders worry their teams will lose the muscle to do hard, un-promptable work. Burke names the mechanism precisely — the body learns to avoid friction and route everything through the model.

For Every, this is a positioning asset, not a threat. The differentiator the team already sells — trainers who actually build — is the antidote: the goal of good AI training isn't "prompt more," it's "know which 20% still needs your judgment." Worth keeping as a quotable opener for the "what AI training should actually change" conversation. Burke's separate same-day note that he's moved "almost completely off Opus onto GPT 5.5" for low-effort work is a useful tell on how power users are now triaging models by task difficulty.
