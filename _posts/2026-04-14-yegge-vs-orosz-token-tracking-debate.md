# Token Tracking Debate: Yegge vs Orosz

**Author:** Steve Yegge (@Steve_Yegge) responding to Gergely Orosz (@GergelyOrosz)
**Date:** 2026-04-14
**URLs:**
- https://x.com/GergelyOrosz/status/2044095388609597588
- https://x.com/Steve_Yegge/status/2044105650603733352

## Tweets

Gergely Orosz:
> Anyone sensible inside the industry is rapidly coming to realize that tokens burned is the most silly thing to track. Real story: talked with a dev inside one of the major AI labs. A team was about to launch an internal token leaderboard. It was promptly stopped with a big WTF

Steve Yegge:
> You can't begin to train and upskill your organization unless you know how many tokens people are burning, Gergely. You're unintentionally giving terrible advice to companies here. YES, they should be tracking token use. NO, they should not be using it as a proxy for productivity.

Also Angie Jones (@techgirl1908):
> measuring token usage was helpful when we were early in our journey and I needed to track adoption. after adoption was clear, I threw it out as it did nothing to measure developer productivity.

## Why This Is Interesting

This is a live debate about one of the most practical questions our clients face: what metrics should you track when rolling out AI? The answer matters:

- **Yegge's point**: You need usage data to understand adoption, identify who needs training, and justify investment. Tracking ≠ gamifying.
- **Orosz's point**: Leaderboards create perverse incentives — people burn tokens to look productive.
- **Angie's synthesis**: Track for adoption phase, then retire as a metric.

## Relevance to Every Consulting

- Direct client question: "How do we measure AI adoption?" — this thread gives us a nuanced three-phase answer
- Angie's framework (track for adoption → drop for productivity) is the right recommendation for our clients
- Brooker's non-tech clients especially need guidance on what to measure vs. what to ignore
