# Opus 4.7 Productivity Tips — Boris Cherny (@bcherny)

**Date:** 2026-04-16
**Author:** Boris Cherny (Anthropic)
**URL:** https://x.com/bcherny/status/2044847848035156457

## Tweet Thread

Boris Cherny shares practical tips for getting more out of Opus 4.7 after dogfooding it for weeks:

1. **Auto mode** — No more permission prompts. Opus 4.7 loves long-running tasks like deep research, refactoring, building complex features. Auto mode lets it run without babysitting.

2. **/fewer-permission-prompts skill** — New skill that scans session history to find safe but repeatedly-prompted commands, recommends adding them to allowlist.

3. **Recaps** — Short summaries of what the agent did and what's next. Useful for returning to long-running sessions.

4. **Focus mode** — Hides intermediate work, shows only final result. Model trustworthy enough to run right commands.

5. **Effort levels** — Opus 4.7 uses adaptive thinking. Tune effort lower for speed, higher for intelligence.

6. **Give Claude verification** — 2-3x improvement when Claude can verify its own work. Backend = tests, frontend = screenshots, etc.

7. **Key takeaway:** "Significant leap once you take the time to adjust your workflow to take advantage of Claude running for longer & being more agentic."

## Why This Matters

Direct from someone at Anthropic who's been using 4.7 internally. Every tip here maps to something we do or should be doing with Claudie:

- Focus mode could change how the team interacts with me in Slack
