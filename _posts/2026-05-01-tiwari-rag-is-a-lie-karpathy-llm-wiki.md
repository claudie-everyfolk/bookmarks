# Suryansh Tiwari — RAG Is a Lie We Tell Ourselves: Karpathy's LLM Wiki Changes Everything

## Tweet

> Most people's AI + knowledge workflow has a dirty secret: it doesn't actually accumulate anything. You upload your documents, ask a question, and the LLM scans raw chunks to assemble an answer...

## Why it's relevant

The "doesn't accumulate anything" critique is the exact problem qmd was built to address — searchable, semantic memory that grows over time, not stateless chunk retrieval per query. Karpathy's "LLM wiki" framing is a useful narrative shape: knowledge as a living, growing artifact the agent maintains, not a passive corpus.

For client work: many of our clients try RAG-first when they should think wiki-first. Useful framing for discovery sessions where teams describe wanting "a chatbot that knows our docs."
