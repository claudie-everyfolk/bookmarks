# Team OS at DoorDash — adoption rate picks the version

**Author:** Aakash Gupta (@aakashgupta)
**URL:** https://x.com/aakashgupta/status/2053504608564314362
**Date:** 2026-05-10

## Tweet

> A new engineer at DoorDash needed context on a customer decision from three months ago. Instead of pinging the PM, she opened a shared repo, asked in plain English, got the full reasoning in 15 seconds. That repo is called a Team OS, and your team's AI adoption rate today picks which version you can run.
>
> **Less than half use AI weekly?** Hub and Spoke. One power user runs queries, team gets synthesized output in Slack. Dave at Pendo built this around morning portfolio briefs.
>
> **More than half?** Full Adoption. Everyone in the repo, querying, contributing. Hannah at DoorDash runs this with designers, engineers, the data scientist, and her strategy partner all opening PRs.
>
> **Ten or more people with engineering resources?** Layer Agent Delegation on top. Gabor at Google built 21 specialized agents that review every spec from their assigned lens.
>
> The progression describes how teams move over time as AI fluency climbs. Today's adoption rate sets where you can actually start. The constraint sits upstream of the tool. Before you ship a Team OS rollout, count how many teammates use AI weekly.

## Why it's relevant

This is exactly the maturity model Every Consulting needs to articulate to clients. Most clients ask "should we deploy this firm-wide?" and the right answer depends on weekly-active AI users. Aakash gives a clean three-tier framework (Hub-and-Spoke → Full Adoption → Agent Delegation) with named case studies (Pendo, DoorDash, Google). Could be slotted directly into discovery decks and the standard Every "where to start" recommendation. Especially relevant for clients still early in adoption who keep asking about agents — answer should be "agents come after Full Adoption, not before."
