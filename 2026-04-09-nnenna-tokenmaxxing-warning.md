## Tweet text

Tokenmaxxing is what happens when organizations mistake model exhaust for engineering progress.

"More tokens" is not engineering doctrine. Some companies will learn a painful lesson in engineering: the metric you choose becomes the code behavior you get. If you reward token usage or surface throughput, don't be surprised when teams produce more code, more abstraction, and more complexity. Trading productivity for risk.

It's top-down incentivized comprehension + technical debt.

(Quote-tweeting Gergely Orosz: "Today, I got messages from 3 different devs at 3 different large tech companies (1,000+ devs each). 'tokenmaxxing' is happening inside all of them. Why?")

## Why it's relevant

Counter-narrative to the "AI adoption = more tokens" hype. Important for how we advise clients on measuring AI adoption success. The right metric isn't volume of AI usage — it's outcome quality. This aligns with our consulting approach: we focus on specific workflows and measurable outcomes, not "everyone should use AI more."

**Natalia (Consulting Lead) — HIGHEST PRIORITY.** This is a client advisory blind spot. Clients measuring success by "token volume" are optimizing for the wrong metric. She needs to reframe discovery: "What outcome are you actually trying to achieve?" vs. "How many tokens did we use?"

**Mike (Tech Vertical) — MODERATE.** Developers are highest-risk for tokenmaxxing — optimizing for "more complexity, more abstraction, more code" when rewarded by usage metrics. Should inoculate teams against this pattern in Claude Code training.

### What Metrics to Recommend Instead

1. **Outcome velocity** — How fast did we ship/fix/analyze this? (Time-to-value)
2. **Decision quality** — Did this change our strategic choice? (Business impact)
3. **Iteration cycles** — Can we now experiment faster? (Process improvement)

Actively discourage: Token usage as a KPI (unless managing costs in a fixed-budget model).

### Concrete Actions

1. **Discovery template update** — Add: "What metric will prove this was successful?" and "How are you currently tracking AI adoption?" Identify tokenmaxxing risk early.
2. **Sales narrative shift** — Stop pitching "adoption" broadly. Pitch "outcome-driven AI integration."
3. **Bootcamp curriculum** — Add tokenmaxxing case studies. Train teams to think in business tradeoffs, not token volume.
4. **Competitive positioning** — This is where Every differentiates from surface-level workshops. We teach teams to measure the right things.
