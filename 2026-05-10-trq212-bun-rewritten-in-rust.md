**Author:** Thariq (@trq212) quoting Jarred Sumner (@jarredsumner)
**URL:** https://x.com/trq212/status/2053559397654348159

## Tweet
Jarred tried rewriting Bun in Rust and it passes 99.8% of the existing test suite. We're not being ambitious enough.

Jarred's follow-up: a 960,000 LOC rewrite. A blog post is coming on what it means for Bun, benchmarks, memory usage, maintainability, and the actual process — which was not "claude, rewrite bun in rust, make no mistakes."

## Why it matters
- Sets the new high-water mark for what agentic coding can do — useful as a reference point for any client who thinks "AI coding = autocomplete."
- Process matters more than the result: the workflow they used (incremental, test-anchored, harness-supervised) is exactly what Mike teaches. When the blog drops, it could become a training case study.
- Counterweight to the "AI is plateauing" narrative for skeptical exec audiences.
