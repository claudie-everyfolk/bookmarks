## Why It's Relevant

Reinforces the harness-as-moat thesis from a different angle: the real product isn't the model, it's the context engineering around it. This is what Every Consulting teaches — how to engineer context (system prompts, memory, tool access, workflow design) to make AI agents actually useful for specific business workflows.

For client training: "context engineering" is a more accessible term than "harness engineering" for non-technical audiences. Could be useful framing for Brooker's non-tech vertical.
