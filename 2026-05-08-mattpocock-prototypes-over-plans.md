# Pocock: prototypes beat plans

**Author:** Matt Pocock (@mattpocockuk)
**Date:** 2026-05-08
**URL:** https://x.com/mattpocockuk/status/2052437310302462173

## Tweet

"The more I replace plans with prototypes, the better the outputs

Who'd have thought that low fidelity prototypes were better than walls of spec

Oh yeah, the entire industry for 20 years

Stop going against decades of knowledge because someone in SF shipped it as a 'mode'"

## Why it's relevant

A pointed counter to the current "always use plan mode" reflex many clients pick up after a Claude Code training. The honest answer for most coding tasks is closer to: try a 50-line prototype, look at what breaks, iterate. Plans add value when the cost of throwing the prototype away is high; for most agent-flow exploration it isn't.

For Mike's tech-track engagements, this is a useful nuance to teach alongside plan-mode demos so clients don't over-rotate. For Nityesh's own work on Claudie infra, it's a reminder that the right cycle is often "ship a janky version of the launchd job, see what breaks, then write the rubric" — not "write the rubric first."

Companion post by same author, https://x.com/mattpocockuk/status/2052042499053453330, sketches a slash-command flow he runs (`/grill-with-docs` → `/handoff` → `/prototype` → `/to-prd` → `/to-issues`). Worth studying as a reference architecture for our own skill chains.
